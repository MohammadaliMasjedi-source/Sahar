# Sahar — Recording Manifest (the REAL solution)

> The app already speaks — today with a **temporary machine voice** (edge-tts,
> Iranian-Persian accent for Dari, generated by `tools/generate-draft-audio.py`).
> That is the bridge, not the destination. **This file is the destination**:
> every line a real human voice needs to record, in order, with its exact
> target filename. The moment a file below exists at its listed path, it wins
> automatically — no code change, no app rebuild. `audio.js` always tries the
> real file first; the machine-voice draft currently sitting at that same path
> is simply overwritten and the honesty banner updates itself.

## How to record (read this first)

1. **Find a quiet room** — no traffic, no fan, no TV in the background. A closet
   full of clothes or a small carpeted room works surprisingly well.
2. **Hold the phone close** — about a hand's width from your mouth. Use the
   phone's own Voice Memo / recorder app (iPhone: Voice Memos; Android: Recorder
   or Google Recorder). No special equipment needed.
3. **Read the line warm, slow, and smiling** — the way you'd say it to a small
   child sitting next to you, not the way you'd read a report. A small pause
   before and after each line makes trimming easier.
4. **One take per line is enough.** If you stumble, just pause and say the whole
   line again from the start — don't worry about editing out mistakes, re-record
   the line instead.
5. **File format:** phones usually record `.m4a`. That is fine — convert to
   `.mp3` before dropping it in (any free online converter, or ask Mo/whoever is
   at the laptop to run `ffmpeg -i in.m4a out.mp3`). `.mp3` is what `audio.js`
   expects.
6. **Where files go:** drop the finished `.mp3` directly into `audio/` (flat,
   no subfolders needed for the Tier-1 packs — see each table below for the
   exact filename) or `audio/fa-AF/` / `audio/en/` for the First-English-Words
   pack. **Exact filename match is what matters** — same name as listed,
   nothing else.
7. **Machine drafts auto-yield.** You do not need to delete anything first —
   the moment your real file lands at the same path, it plays instead of the
   draft. If a filename doesn't already exist as a draft, that's fine too —
   it just means that specific card wasn't part of this generation batch yet.
8. **Record in whatever order feels natural.** Doing one whole pack in one
   sitting (5-8 lines) is a nice, short session — most packs take under 10
   minutes to record end-to-end.

**Whose voice:** ideally **Mo's or Neda's** — native Dari speakers, and it
makes Sahar warm and personal. Ideally the *same* voice throughout one pack
(a child will notice a stitched-together lesson). English/German lines are
optional extras — the Dari (`fa`) line is what matters most, since that's the
child's mother tongue and the one no honest browser TTS can currently do at all.

## Totals
- **67 Tier-1 prompt lines** across 9 packs (full sentences/questions, ~2-15
  seconds each when read naturally).
- **10 single words** for the First-English-Words pack (1-2 seconds each).
- **77 lines total** for a complete real-voice pass in Dari. English (67 + 10)
  and German (67) versions of the same content are optional, lower-priority
  extras — every card also has a machine-voice draft in those languages
  already, and browser TTS genuinely works for both, so a "wrong voice" is a
  much smaller honesty problem there than it is for Dari.
- Recording all 77 Dari lines in short sittings (one pack at a time, ~5-15
  minutes each) is a realistic weekend project, not an all-at-once ask.
