# Sahar Funding, Sponsorship, Partnerships, and Media Strategy

**Prepared:** 2026-07-12, Europe/Berlin  
**Scope:** Sahar as it exists today; Germany/EU-first funding routes; no application or outreach was submitted  
**Source rule:** Current official programme pages were checked on 2026-07-12. Deadlines and eligibility must be rechecked immediately before submission.

## Executive answer

Sahar can become fundable, but the strongest route today is **not “ask a big foundation for app money.”** Sahar is a public and serious prototype, but it does not yet have the four things institutional funders usually need:

1. a legal or fiscal recipient for grants and donations;
2. a safeguarded pilot partner with access to real learners;
3. independent content/voice review by Dari educators;
4. evidence from a real-device, adult-supervised pilot.

The recommended strategy is therefore:

> **Use startsocial immediately for organizational coaching; use TU Clausthal and one established Afghan-education NGO to create the legal/pilot bridge; fund one small, auditable pilot before pursuing large grants or national media.**

The project should remain free, ad-free, tracking-free, and open source. Money should fund validated units—Dari review, human voice, device testing, safeguarding, pilot facilitation, and illustrations—not access to children and never advertising inside the learner experience.

## The credibility position

The current funder-safe sentence is:

> **Sahar is a free, open-source, offline-first learning prototype for children with interrupted or denied access to schooling. It currently offers a multilingual Tier-1 foundation and is seeking expert review and an adult-supervised pilot; it does not yet claim measured learning impact or readiness for unsupervised deployment.**

Do not currently claim:

- “ready for Afghan learners”;
- “proven to improve learning”;
- “safe for covert use in Afghanistan”;
- “unique” or “the first”;
- “Dari voice complete”;
- “UN curriculum approved”;
- “partnered with UNICEF/UNESCO/any NGO”;
- a number of beneficiaries.

UNESCO and UNICEF’s January 2026 joint statement reports that Afghanistan is the only country where girls and women are forbidden from secondary and higher education, with 2.2 million adolescent girls affected and a severe early-grade learning crisis. This supports the need, but it does **not** validate Sahar’s solution or authorize deployment. Source: [UNESCO/UNICEF joint statement](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/unesco-and-unicef-urge-action-protect-right-education-afghanistan).

## Recommended operating model

### Track A — public-benefit initiative through a partner (recommended now)

Sahar remains an open, not-for-profit initiative. A suitable German nonprofit, university/public institution, or fiscal host receives restricted funds and holds the pilot agreement. Mo remains the product steward; educators and the partner own learner safety and field validation.

Advantages:

- aligned with the current README promise;
- grant and donation receipts become possible through the partner;
- access to safeguarded learners and professional educators;
- avoids forming an organization before the partnership model is understood;
- strongest credibility for foundations and media.

Risks:

- partner due diligence and shared control;
- slower decisions;
- written agreement needed on software/content licensing, data, branding, safeguarding, ownership, and termination.

### Track B — social enterprise/startup (only if Mo deliberately changes direction)

Core learner access could remain free while institutions pay for deployment, customization, training, support, or verified content services. This could unlock EXIST or the Niedersachsen Gründungsstipendium.

This is **not** compatible with the current “Sahar is not a business” wording unless the mission and funding model are formally revised. It also interacts with Mo’s employment, residence permit, employer-consent, tax, and working-time position.

### Track C — individual open-source sponsorship

GitHub Sponsors can accept one-time/monthly support for open-source maintenance in Germany. It is a useful later channel, but it is not a substitute for a pilot partner or grant recipient. Payout setup requires personal identity, bank, tax information, and 2FA. Receiving funds may trigger residence/employer/tax gates.

## Immediate opportunity: startsocial

Why it is the best first application:

- accepts individuals and teams without legal form or charity status;
- explicitly covers education, migration/integration, participation, and gender equality;
- accepts idea-stage and established initiatives;
- evaluates effectiveness, sustainability, efficiency, transferability, and the leverage of coaching—the exact areas Sahar needs;
- every applicant receives written jury feedback;
- selected projects receive two coaches from 2 November 2026 to 28 February 2027;
- selected initiatives receive visibility; finalists can receive part of €35,000 in prizes.

Weakness to handle honestly:

Sahar does not yet have an active volunteer community. startsocial requires significant volunteer involvement or the motivation of volunteers. The application must present a real, bounded volunteer model—not imply volunteers already exist.

Proposed volunteer roles:

1. Dari educator/content reviewer;
2. Dari voice contributor;
3. child-safeguarding/accessibility reviewer;
4. translator/localizer;
5. open-source developer/tester;

## The first fundable unit: a safeguarded pilot

### Objective

Test whether a Dari-speaking child and caregiver can install/open Sahar on a low-end phone, understand the first interactions, use it offline, and return to it—without collecting unnecessary personal data.

### Safe pilot sequence

1. Three adult expert reviews: Dari educator, child-safeguarding professional, accessibility/usability reviewer.
2. Real-device airplane-mode and PWA-install verification on at least three low-end Android devices.
3. Human Dari audio for the 85-line manifest, with written voice-use consent.
4. Adult-supervised usability pilot with 3–5 children in Germany through a qualified partner and parental/guardian consent.
5. Fix critical content, pronunciation, safety, and usability findings.
6. Only then design a larger 30–60 learner study with ethics/data-protection review.

The first pilot measures usability and acceptability, **not learning impact**.

## Outreach protocol

1. **QUALIFY:** official source, deadline, applicant type, geography, legal entity, co-finance, prior-start rule, employment conflict.
2. **EVIDENCE:** attach exact Sahar proof for every claim.
3. **GATE:** confirm pilot, legal, security, rights, and public-claim gates.
4. **DRAFT:** tailor one ask to one recipient; never bulk spam.
5. **APPROVE:** Mo approves recipient + final wording + any personal claims.
6. **SEND:** log date, sender, version, and attachments.
7. **FOLLOW UP:** once after 7–10 business days; once more only if there is new evidence.
8. **CLOSE:** outcome, reason, next eligibility date, and relationship notes.
