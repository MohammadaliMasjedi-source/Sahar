## English

Dear Sir or Madam, `[MO: use a personal salutation if you can confirm the right contact person]`

Your work of more than 40 years for schools and for the education of Afghan children carries real weight for me, which is exactly why I am writing to you — not to ask for money, but to ask for an honest, practice-based assessment.

My name is `[MO: full name]` and, in my own time, I build **Sahar**, a free, open-source, offline-first learning prototype for children with interrupted or denied access to schooling. Sahar currently offers a multilingual Tier-1 foundation (Dari/Persian, English, German), runs without internet after the first load, and needs no account, shows no ads, and collects no data.

I want to stay honest: Sahar is a prototype. There is no independent educational review yet, the Dari voice is so far only a machine-generated draft, and there has been no supervised pilot with children. I claim no learning impact and no readiness for deployment — and I see Sahar at most as a small, supplementary option, never a replacement for the teaching and the structures you have built on the ground.

So my specific request: would you be open to a short conversation in which, from your field experience, you judge:

- whether the content and approach fit the real lives and learning of Afghan children at all,
- which cultural, linguistic, or child-safeguarding mistakes stand out to you immediately,
- and whether an offline supplementary tool would have any sensible place in your work — or rather not?

Public repository (code MIT, content CC-BY-SA 4.0): https://github.com/MohammadaliMasjedi-source/Sahar

Honest criticism — including a firm "this does not work as it is" — would be genuinely valuable to me. Thank you for your time and for your work.
