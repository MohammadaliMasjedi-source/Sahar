## 0. Why this fund, and the honest fit

Unlike almost every other cultural-funding door in Mo's funding map, Landkreis Goslar's
Kulturförderung explicitly invites **individuals** and **small first-time applicants** —
it does not require a registered Verein, does not require prior grant experience, and it
is **local by design** (has to serve/benefit the district). Clausthal-Zellerfeld, where
Mo lives, is inside Landkreis Goslar, so a project rooted there is a natural fit, not a
stretch.

The honest fit question: this is a small **district** fund (€70k/year total, now
routinely capped near €5k per project). It rewards a **concrete, local, modestly-scoped**
cultural or educational activity — a workshop series, a small event, a local
presentation — not a big multi-year program. So this draft deliberately proposes
something **realistic for €5,000–€8,000**, not a scaled-down version of Sahar's full
vision.

## 1. The proposed project (concrete, local, realistic scope)

**Working title:** *Sahar-Werkstatt Clausthal — ein Kultur- und Bildungs-Workshop für
Kinder mit Fluchtgeschichte* `[MO: final title is yours]`

**One-line description (German):**
Eine kleine, lokale Workshop-Reihe in Clausthal-Zellerfeld, in der Kinder mit
unterbrochenem Schulzugang (u. a. neu zugewanderte Kinder) gemeinsam mit einer
pädagogischen Begleitperson Inhalte für **Sahar** — eine freie, offline nutzbare
Lern-App in Dari/Persisch, Englisch und Deutsch — mitgestalten: echte Sprachaufnahmen,
einfache Illustrationen und das Erproben der App im Offline-Betrieb. Ein kleines,
öffentliches Abschluss-Ereignis in Clausthal zeigt die Ergebnisse der lokalen
Öffentlichkeit.

**Why this fits a district cultural fund specifically:**
- It happens **physically in Clausthal-Zellerfeld / Landkreis Goslar** — not remotely,
  not only online.
- It is **participatory culture-making** (children as co-creators, not passive users) —
  exactly the kind of small, community-level cultural activity the program names as
  eligible (*"Theaterworkshops für Kinder"* is literally the district's own example
  category).
- It **directly benefits local children** (new arrivals, refugee families already living
  in the district) while producing something openly reusable — a genuinely
  community-level outcome, not a pretext.
- It is **modest and one-off**, matching the fund's now-typical €5,000 grant size, not
  an ongoing operating cost the district would have to keep funding.

## 2. Content of the project — what actually happens

A **~4–6 month, single-round** local workshop project:

1. **Partner and child-safety setup (Month 1).** Agree a hosting partner in Clausthal or
   nearby Goslar (a Jugendzentrum, family/integration centre, or school willing to host)
   `[MO: partner still to be found/confirmed — see decision list]`. No work with children
   starts before a written consent + supervision framework (Erziehungsberechtigte,
   Fachaufsicht) is in place.
2. **Workshop series (Month 2–4).** A small group of children (realistically 6–15 kids,
   sized to the budget) takes part in **4–6 sessions**: recording real human Dari/Farsi
   voice lines for Sahar's existing 85-line recording script, drawing simple
   illustrations, and testing the app hands-on in offline mode on borrowed tablets/phones.
3. **Light professional support.** Small honoraria for a **Dari-speaking facilitator**
   and a **child-safety-aware workshop lead**, so the sessions are linguistically and
   pedagogically sound, not improvised.
4. **Small public closing event (Month 5).** A modest, safe local presentation (e.g. at
   the partner venue or a small community space in Clausthal) where the children's work
   is shown to families and the local community — no politicisation, no exposure risk to
   any individual child beyond what guardians consent to.
5. **Open publication.** The resulting audio/content is published under Sahar's existing
   open licenses (MIT code / CC-BY-SA 4.0 content) so it benefits Sahar's global mission,
   not just this one local round.

**What already exists (honest, from the repo today):** a working offline-first
prototype; ten Tier-1 learning packs plus a language-course pack in Dari/Persian (RTL),
English, and German; a Leitner spaced-repetition engine; 245 temporary machine-generated
audio clips, honestly flagged in-app as draft voice; an 85-line Dari recording script
ready to use; 132 passing automated checks; MIT/CC-BY-SA open licenses; a public
repository.

**What does not exist yet and this project would create:** real human Dari/Farsi voice
recordings (replacing the temporary machine voice); a tested, supervised local pilot with
real children; a local partner relationship; any measured outcome data.

## 5. Kinderschutz & Do-No-Harm (non-negotiable)

- Work with children only through a qualified local partner with proper supervision and
  **written guardian consent**.
- No personal learning data collected, no advertising, no tracking, no forced accounts.
- No public identification of individual children without explicit, informed guardian
  consent; anonymize by default.
- **Zero politics:** no Iran/Afghanistan political content or messaging — strictly
  education and culture. Child and family safety comes first.
- Sahar stays privacy-by-default and secular (no religious symbols), so participation
  never puts anyone at risk.
