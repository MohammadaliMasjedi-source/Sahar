## English

Dear Visions for Children team,

I am writing because I greatly respect your long-standing work on education in Afghanistan, and what I am really looking for is an honest expert view — not money, and not a partnership commitment.

My name is `[MO: full name]` and, in my own time, I build **Sahar**, a free, open-source, offline-first learning prototype for children with interrupted or denied access to schooling. Sahar currently offers a multilingual Tier-1 foundation (Dari/Persian, English, German), works without internet after the first load, and uses no account, no ads, and no data collection.

I want to be careful not to overclaim: this is a prototype. There is no independent educational or Dari-language review yet, the Dari audio is so far only machine-generated draft voice, there has been no supervised pilot with children, and I claim no measured learning impact and no readiness for deployment — least of all for use inside Afghanistan. Sahar is meant as a possible narrow, child-facing complement, never a replacement for the work of experienced education organizations.

My request is deliberately small. Would you be open to roughly a 30-minute conversation in which you critically reflect back to me:

- whether my framing of the problem is realistic and appropriate,
- which content, ethical, and child-safeguarding risks stand out to you immediately,
- and whether an independent expert review, or a very small supervised usability test with Dari-speaking families in Germany, would even make sense?

Public repository (code MIT, content CC-BY-SA 4.0): https://github.com/MohammadaliMasjedi-source/Sahar

Even a clear "we don't have capacity" or "not in this form" would be valuable to me. Thank you very much for your time.

Kind regards,
