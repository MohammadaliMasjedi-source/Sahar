# Sahar — Curriculum Map

*The whole body of knowledge a UN-"educated person" should hold, mapped subject-by-subject to the
four age tiers as **phases / content packs.** This is the spine the packs hang on. Concrete on
purpose — vague curricula don't ship.*

> **Reference frame.** "Educated person" here = the floor implied by UNESCO/UN SDG-4 and a typical
> national basic-education core: functional **literacy & numeracy**, the **sciences** at Crash-Course
> breadth, **critical thinking**, **digital literacy**, **health & the body**, **civics / economy /
> politics basics**, **survival/life skills**, **arts & literature**, and a **window into world
> languages**. A learner who completes all four tiers should clear that floor and then some.

Each cell below is a **pack family** (`tier.subject.topic`, versioned, per ARCHITECTURE §4). Packs are
small and downloaded per tier, so a 7-year-old's device holds only Tier-1.

## Tier 1 — age 7 · *"I can begin."*
*Goal: the doorway. Letters become sounds, sounds become words; numbers become quantities; the child
learns that a **question** is a thing you can ask.*

| Subject | What's in the pack |
|---|---|
| **Literacy (Dari/Persian first)** | The alphabet by sound (phonics); letter ↔ sound; first 50 sight words; left-to-right *and* right-to-left tracking; "my name". Audio-first. |
| **Numeracy** | Counting 0–20; more/fewer/equal; number ↔ quantity; first addition by counting; shapes. |
| **Critical thinking (seed)** | **"What is a question?"** Asking vs. telling. "Same vs. different." Sorting by one rule. |
| **Science (wonder)** | Living vs. not-living; day & night (the dawn!); hot/cold, wet/dry; my five senses. |
| **Health & body** | Body parts; washing hands; safe vs. unsafe; "this is my body". |
| **Arts** | Colors; drawing; one song; one folk story listened to. |
| **Digital (gentle)** | This is a screen; tap, swipe, the home button; "a device is a tool". |
| **Sahar-companion** | Sahar learns her letters *with you*; introduces icon-navigation. |

## Tier 2 — age 8–9 · *"I can read and reckon."*
*Goal: independent reading of simple sentences; arithmetic with confidence; the world has patterns.*

| Subject | What's in the pack |
|---|---|
| **Literacy** | Reading & writing sentences; 300 common words; question vs. statement; retell a short story; first paragraph. Begin **English** as a foreign language (alphabet, greetings). |
| **Numeracy** | Addition & subtraction fluency; intro multiplication & division; place value to 1000; money & making change; telling time; basic measurement. |
| **Critical thinking** | **Fact vs. opinion** (the prototype's lesson lives here); cause & effect; "why do you think that?"; spotting a guess vs. a known. |
| **Science** | Plants & animals & habitats; the water cycle; weather; states of matter; the Sun, Moon, Earth; simple machines. |
| **Geography / world** | Map of my country & the world; continents & oceans; my town vs. far away. |
| **Health & body** | Healthy food; teeth; sleep; germs & staying well; emotions have names. |
| **Digital literacy** | What the internet is; "not everything on a screen is true" (seed); kind vs. unkind online. |
| **Arts & music** | Rhythm; primary vs. secondary colors; a poem to recite; a story to act out. |
| **Sahar-companion** | Sahar reads a sentence *with you* and asks "is that a fact or what you think?" |

## Tier 3 — age 10–11 · *"I can understand how things work."*
*Goal: paragraphs and ideas; fractions and ratios; first real science; the past explains the present.*

| Subject | What's in the pack |
|---|---|
| **Literacy** | Reading paragraphs & short non-fiction; main idea & summary; writing a paragraph with a point; dictionary use. **English** to working basics; **German** introduced (greetings, alphabet). |
| **Numeracy** | Fractions, decimals, percentages; ratios; multi-digit ×/÷; area & perimeter; intro to negative numbers; reading a simple chart. |
| **Critical thinking** | **Evidence vs. claim**; correlation isn't cause (gently); reliable vs. unreliable source; how to check a fact. |
| **Science (Crash-Course breadth begins)** | Cells & the body's systems; forces & energy; matter & simple chemistry; the solar system; ecosystems & food chains; **how scientists ask questions** (observe → guess → test). |
| **History & civics** | Timelines; ancient → modern in broad strokes; my country's story (told plainly, multiple sides); "what is a rule, who makes it?" |
| **Economy (seed)** | Wants vs. needs; saving; what money is; trade & exchange. |
| **Digital literacy** | Searching well; what a source is; privacy & passwords; **deepfakes/edited images exist** (seed). |
| **Survival / life skills** | First aid basics; water safety; fire safety; reading a map; basic budgeting of a small amount. |
| **Arts & literature** | A short story analyzed; perspective in drawing; world music; write a short poem. |
| **Sahar-companion** | Sahar runs a tiny experiment with you and asks "what's your evidence?" |

## Tier 4 — age 12–13–14 · *"I can think for myself."*
*Goal: the floor of an educated adult — abstract math, the scientific method, her own body, money,
citizenship, and the ability to not be fooled.*

| Subject | What's in the pack |
|---|---|
| **Literacy & expression** | Essays with thesis & evidence; argument & counter-argument; reading literature critically; note-taking; **English & German to conversational/working level**; a window into one more world language. |
| **Mathematics** | **Algebra basics** (variables, equations, linear functions); geometry & proof intuition; ratio/proportion mastery; **statistics & probability** (average, spread, "what a percentage hides"); intro to graphs/functions. |
| **The scientific method** | Hypothesis → experiment → data → conclusion → peer check; controls & variables; **biology** (genetics, evolution, the body), **chemistry** (atoms, reactions), **physics** (motion, energy, electricity), **Earth & space** at Crash-Course breadth. |
| **Critical thinking & logic** | Logical fallacies; **media literacy** — propaganda, bias, how headlines manipulate; statistics used to lie; **science and belief** handled gently and respectfully (companion book below); how to change your mind. |
| **Health & the body** | **Puberty & adolescent development** (honest, age-appropriate, body-positive); nutrition; mental health & stress; consent & safety; reproductive basics (factual, dignified). |
| **Civics / politics / economy** | What a government does; rights & responsibilities; **personal finance** (budgeting, saving, interest, scams); how markets & jobs work; what an election is; human rights — incl. **the right to education**. |
| **Digital literacy** | Misinformation & verification; account & device security; algorithms shape what you see; a footprint is forever; basic intro to *how* code works. |
| **Survival / life skills** | Practical first aid; planning & decision-making; navigating institutions; self-defense of one's rights; resilience. |
| **Arts & literature** | A novel & a play; art movements; create & critique; literature from her own culture and the world. |
| **Sahar-companion** | Sahar debates a media headline with you — "is this evidence, or are they selling something?" |

## Companion short illustrated books
Standalone, beautifully illustrated, ~20–40 pages each, readable on their own — the "soul" shelf.
Delivered as their own packs.

1. **"How to Think"** — critical thinking for kids: questions, evidence, changing your mind, fallacies
   you can spot. (T2 seed → T4 depth.)
2. **"Why the Sky Does That"** — science & wonder; **science and belief handled gently** — *how we
   find things out*, never mocking faith, never telling a child what to believe; "here is how we
   know, and it's okay to wonder." (T3–T4.)
3. **"Sahar and the Locked Door"** — friendship & courage; Sahar's own story; not alone. (All tiers.)
4. **"What Money Is"** — economy for kids: trade, saving, scams, fairness. (T3–T4.)
5. **"If You're Ever Lost"** — survival & safety: first aid, water, fire, finding your way, asking for
   help. (T3–T4.)

## How this maps to packs & phases
- Each cell = one or more `tier.subject.topic` pack families (ARCHITECTURE §4), versioned, per-tier.
- **Build order:** Tier-1 literacy + numeracy + the *fact-vs-opinion* critical-thinking seed first
  (the prototype already demos that seed), then widen across subjects, then climb tiers.
- A learner only ever stores the packs for her current tier(s); finishing a tier unlocks the next.

> Honest status: this map is the **plan**. The content **built** today is seven Tier-1 packs in
> `content/` (first letters, counting 0–20, shapes & patterns, living things, "what is a question?",
> "guess first, then check", and the fact-vs-opinion demo), each in fa/en/de. Everything else is
> specified here and not yet authored.
