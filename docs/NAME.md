# Sahar — the Name & the Heroine

*The brand name **and** the soul of the app. **Mo's final call** — this is a recommendation, argued
honestly.*

---

## Recommendation: **Sahar (سحر)** — "dawn"

**Sahar** means *dawn* — specifically the **last dark hour before sunrise**, the moment in Persian and
Dari poetry when night is heaviest but light is certain. It is a common, beautiful **girl's name**
across Afghanistan, Iran, and the wider Persianate world. For an app built for girls told their
future is dark, a name that means *"the light is coming"* is not decoration — it is the thesis.

Why it wins:
- **One word, two meanings:** the literal dawn (hope, beginning, a new day of learning) and a real
  girl's name (so the app *is* a girl).
- **Pronounceable everywhere** — sah-HAR — in fa/dari, en, de, and beyond. Short, warm, dignified.
- **Secular.** Dawn belongs to no religion; it belongs to everyone who waits out a long night.
- **Scales across editions:** one feminine brand (*Sahar*) can wear a country edition's flag without
  losing identity — *Sahar Afghanistan*, *Sahar* (global), the heroine is the same girl.
- **Palette is built in:** deep night → amber sunrise is literally the app's dawn color system.

## The shortlist (all real, all considered)

| Name | Script | Meaning | Why / why not |
|------|--------|---------|---------------|
| **Sahar** ✅ | سحر | **dawn, daybreak** | *Recommended.* Hope + a girl's name + secular + scales. The thesis in one word. |
| Sepideh | سپیده | dawn's first whiteness / first light | Gorgeous, same dawn family; slightly harder for non-Persian tongues; less common as a short brand. |
| Parastoo | پرستو | **swallow** (the bird) | Warm, girl's name, evokes spring's return & migration (refugees) — lovely but indirect; "swallow" doesn't say *learning*. |
| Roshani | روشنی | **light / brightness** | Strong meaning (enlightenment, literally), clearly secular; a touch abstract as a *character's* name — light is a thing, Sahar is a girl. |

All four are feminine, hopeful, and secular. **Sahar** uniquely is *both a person and the dawn*, which
is exactly what this app needs to be.

## Sahar is also the heroine — the heart, not a logo

This is the idea worth developing: **Sahar is not just the app's name — she is a girl who lives
inside it.**

> Sahar is a girl who was in school until the door was locked. She is the same age as whoever opens
> the app. She didn't give up — she found another way to learn, and now she learns **alongside you**.
> When you start a lesson, she starts it too. When you get a card right, she cheers. When you miss
> one, she shrugs and says *"again — that's how I learn too."* She grows up tier by tier as the user
> does: at 7 she's learning her letters with you; at 13 she's arguing about whether a claim is a fact
> or an opinion, right beside you.

This is the **Duolingo-owl role — but with a soul.** Duo is a nagging mascot; **Sahar is a friend who
was denied the same thing you were and refused to stop.** She is:

- **a mirror** — she is the user, given a face, so a lonely girl learning in secret is not alone;
- **a guide** — she models each new action once (icon-first, see ARCHITECTURE §7), in-character;
- **a witness** — her existence says *"someone like you made it through this; so can you."*
- **secular and safe** — a hopeful girl facing a sunrise; **no religious symbols** (ARCHITECTURE §10).

### Visual identity
- **Dawn palette:** deep night indigo → amber/rose sunrise. Sahar stands small against a brightening
  sky — the night behind her, the light on her face.
- Inline-SVG so she renders **offline**, themeable, and can take simple poses (cheering, thinking,
  encouraging) as the character system grows.
- One feminine brand mark across all country editions — the *same girl*, optionally with a local
  flag/landscape behind her, never a different mascot per country.

### One brand, many editions
Sahar is the constant. Editions (*Sahar — Afghanistan edition*, future country editions) change the
**packs and the backdrop, never the girl.** A diaspora donor funding the Afghan edition and a child
opening the global one meet the *same Sahar*. That single, recognizable, feminine identity is the
brand's strength — and the reason to pick a real girl's name over an abstract one.

---

**Final name and whether Sahar-the-character ships as the mascot is Mo's call.** This document
recommends **yes to both**: name it *Sahar*, and let Sahar be the girl who learns beside every child
who opens it.
