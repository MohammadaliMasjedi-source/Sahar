# License — how it was decided

Sahar is free forever and not-for-profit. It carries a **dual license**, chosen so the engine
travels far and the lessons stay free:

- **Code → MIT** (see [`LICENSE`](LICENSE)). Maximally permissive so anyone — another NGO, another
  diaspora, another country's denied children — can fork the engine and build their own Sahar.
  The point is reach, not control.
- **Original content & learning packs → CC-BY-SA 4.0** (see [`LICENSE-CONTENT.md`](LICENSE-CONTENT.md)).
  Free to use and adapt, must credit, and any derivative stays free under the same terms. This
  protects the work from being enclosed or sold.

**Third-party Open Educational Resources** drawn into packs keep **their own** licenses (CC-BY,
CC-BY-SA, CC-BY-NC, public domain), recorded per pack in the pack's `license`/`attribution` fields —
see [`docs/OSS-REUSE.md`](docs/OSS-REUSE.md).
